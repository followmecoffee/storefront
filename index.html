The Journey In Every Cup

Beans with a redemption story

We source from two origins where coffee didn't just grow an economy — it rewrote one. Knowing the farmer from soil to cup isn't a marketing line for us. It's the whole point.

Thailand · Doi Chang

From poppy fields to coffee farms

In the highlands of Northern Thailand, hill-tribe families once farmed opium to survive. A decades-long effort replaced those fields with arabica and reforested the mountains in the process. The families who were once stateless are now world-class growers — and our partners.

Mountains reforested to grow shade canopy  ·  Direct trade with hill-tribe farmers
India · Western Ghats

Coffee that protects a rainforest

Indian coffee is grown beneath a living canopy — original jungle trees up to 100 feet tall, intercropped with pepper and cardamom. It yields less than sun-grown farms, but it shelters elephants, hornbills, and the families of smallholders who tend it by hand.

Over 95% grown by small & marginal farmers  ·  shade-grown, bird-friendly
